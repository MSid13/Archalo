We're trying our best

Honest talk about how we handle data — and where we're still improving

The Reality

We're not perfect. Archaeological data is complex, regulations change, and contact information goes out of date. But we're committed to being as accurate as possible.

We verify information by calling cities and counties, meeting with archaeologists across the coasts, and cross-checking multiple sources. When we find errors or outdated info, we fix them. When users report issues, we investigate immediately.

What We Actually Do

Here's how we try to keep things accurate (even when it's hard)

We Call Around

We contact cities and counties to verify contact info and protocols. Sometimes they don't pick up. Sometimes info changes the next week. We do our best to stay current.

Multiple Check-Ins

We cross-reference official sources and check things multiple times. But we're human — mistakes happen. That's why we fix them quickly when they're found.

Real Conversations

We've met with archaeologists across both coasts to understand local practices. These conversations help, but coverage is still growing. We don't have relationships everywhere yet.

Regular Updates

Laws and contacts change constantly. We try to catch updates, but we can't monitor everything 24/7. If something's wrong, let us know.

Source Tracking

We trace info back to government sites and official sources. When possible. Sometimes sources conflict or are unclear. We do our best to interpret them correctly.

You Keep Us Honest

User reports are critical. When you tell us something's wrong, we investigate and fix it. Your feedback makes this tool better for everyone.

Our Coverage (The Honest Version)

We've met with archaeologists on both coasts and built some relationships with local offices in those regions. It's a start, but we're nowhere near complete coverage.

Every connection takes time — calling offices, scheduling meetings, verifying information. We're expanding, but it's slow work. If your area isn't well-covered yet, we're sorry. We're working on it.
